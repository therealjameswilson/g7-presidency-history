04 · Questions for planners

Use history to sharpen decisions, not to prescribe them.

Agenda architecture

What belongs at leaders’ level?

Which inherited issues need a leaders’ decision, which need a ministerial track, and which are better handled through another forum?

Continuity

Which commitments require visible follow-through?

Identify prior declarations with implementation horizons that cross into 2027, and name the evidence needed to judge progress.

Coalition

Who needs to be in the room?

Past presidencies used partner invitations to connect the G7 to regions, institutions, and implementation capacity beyond the seven members.

Deliverables

What document form fits the objective?

Compare omnibus communiqués, focused declarations, action plans, charters, and chair’s summaries before deciding how agreement should be recorded.

Sequencing

How should ministerial work feed the summit?

Map the evidence, negotiation, and decision points backward from the leaders’ meeting rather than treating the summit as a standalone event.

Durability

What survives a change in conditions?

Test proposed commitments against market shocks, security crises, leadership transitions, and disagreement among members.
