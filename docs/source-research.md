# Source research and caveats

Last live verification: **10 July 2026**

This note records the source strategy behind `data/summits.json`, a 52-record annual chronology from the 1975 Rambouillet G6 meeting through the completed 15–17 June 2026 G7 summit at Évian-les-Bains.

## Scope and unit of analysis

- The unit is the principal annual presidency-year/leaders-level record, not every ministerial, Sherpa, outreach, or extraordinary virtual meeting held during a presidency.
- There is one record for every year from 1975 through 2026.
- The April 1996 Moscow Nuclear Safety and Security Summit (G7 with Russia) is noted in the Lyon record but not counted as a second annual summit.
- Extraordinary leaders’ calls on Afghanistan, Ukraine, COVID-19, and other crises remain outside the series unless needed to explain the annual record.
- For 2020, the dataset uses the 16 March U.S.-hosted virtual leaders’ meeting as the substantive annual-cycle meeting and records that the planned 10–12 June Camp David summit was postponed indefinitely and never held.

## Source hierarchy

1. Official host-government or host-institution sources are preferred when available (`tier: 1`).
2. University of Toronto G7 Research Group copies of official summit documents provide continuity for the historical series (`tier: 2`). They reproduce official texts but are not the issuing government.
3. Institutional histories and scholarship may cross-check dates, transitions, and editorial summaries; they do not replace the linked summit text.

## Controlled policy-thread vocabulary

The `themes` field uses ten stable editorial tags: Macroeconomic coordination; Trade; Energy and resources; Development and global partnerships; Peace and security; Global health; Technology; Democracy and society; Environment; and Institutional governance.

The more specific agenda formulations appear under `outcomes`. Theme assignment supports discovery; it is not an official taxonomy and does not measure salience, implementation, or compliance.

## Edge cases

- **G6/G7/G8:** 1975 is G6. Canada joined in 1976. Russia’s participation expanded progressively; Birmingham 1998 begins the regular G8-labeled annual series used here. Some financial work continued as G7 during the G8 era.
- **2014:** Russia was due to host at Sochi. After the annexation of Crimea, the other seven suspended G8 participation and the European Union hosted a G7 meeting in Brussels.
- **2018:** The Charlevoix communiqué was released as a consensus text, but President Trump withdrew U.S. endorsement after leaving. The record names the complication.
- **2019, 2025, and 2026:** These years did not use a conventional long omnibus communiqué; the record distinguishes leaders’ declarations, issue texts, and chair’s summaries.
- **Venues:** A null venue is deliberate where a precise building or resort was not confidently established.
- **Summaries:** `summary`, `context`, `outcomes`, and `why_it_matters` are editorial aids and paraphrases, not quotations.
- **Ordinal treatment:** The sequence counts the 2020 presidency cycle even though no in-person summit occurred, preserving the conventional count that identifies 2026 as the 52nd annual cycle.
- **Link durability:** Host-presidency websites are often retired. Link health should be rechecked before a major release or archival capture.
